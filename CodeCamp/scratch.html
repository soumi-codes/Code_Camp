Scratch

Scratch is an interactive programming language designed to teach children mathematical and computational ideas through creative thinking. It was developed at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT) Media Lab for computation in an interactive way with visual design. Scratch tool is intended for all levels in secondary and undergraduate learners with the goal of learning mathematical and computational ideas through artistic, open-ended play. In Scratch it does not require a high-level coding skill or discourage users who donâ€™t have a natural like-mindedness for the sciences in programming. The platform itself functions as blank canvas for creation, which is inviting to all ages, genders, self-perceived skill, and natural talents.

Scratch Programming Learning Outcome:
Identify the function of common tools in scratch user interface. Scratch programming environment. Three panes: stage, sprite list, and scripts tab.
Function of different block tabs which divided into 10 palettes: motion, looks, sounds, pen, data, sensing, events, control, operators, and more blocks.
On the stage controls:
Sprite moves, interact, and draw
Show the status of the project
Presentation mode (hide the tools)
The green flag to start, and red circle to stop
Add background image called a backdrop.
Dragging blocks from the block tabs and snapping them together. Disassemble stack of blocks to test individual block.
Detach a block in the middle of the stack, grab and drag.
Play sounds, like missile hits or misses the target. Add sounds from MP3 and from other media. Edit the effect of the sounds like fade in or fade out sounds.
